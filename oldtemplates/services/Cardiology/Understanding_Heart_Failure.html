{# TITLE: Understanding Heart Failure #}

What is Heart Failure?

Heart failure is the inability of the heart to pump blood effectively. This does not mean that the heart stops working completely.

Weakening of the heart muscle causes heart failure. This can result from high blood pressure, previous heart attacks, valve disease or other problems.

What You Can Do:

  • Follow your doctor's advice.
  • Take medications as prescribed.
  • Maintain a healthy low salt, low fat diet.
  • Exercise as instructed by your doctor.
  • Weigh yourself daily and fill out symptom log.
  • Avoid stress.
  • Keep your appointments.

What are the Warning Signs?

  • Shortness of breath
  • Feeling tired, run-down
  • Swelling in feet, ankles and legs
  • Weight gain from fluid build-up
  • Cough
  • Loss of appetite

Call your Doctor or Nurse if you Experience:

  • Weight gain
  • Loss of appetite
  • Increased shortness of breath
  • Persistent cough
  • Frequent urination at night
  • Swelling of feet, legs or abdomen
